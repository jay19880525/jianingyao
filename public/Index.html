Jianing(Jay) YAO

Ph.D in Operations Research, Rutgers University, State University of New Jersey

I am a doctoral student of Operations Resarch in Rutgers University. Under supervision of Prof. Andrzej Ruszczyński , I mainly study stochastic optimization theory, numerical methods and applications. In particular, I work on continuous-time optimal control with risk-aversion via forwad backward stochastic differential equations(FBSDE). Besides, I am also interested in financial engineering, specialized in fixed income and credit risk modeling. As hobby, I like programming for artificial intelligence and machine learning.

Currently, I am doing my internship, in Numerix, LLC, as a financial engineer. I was doing researches on the model risk caused by prevailing negative rates in Japan and some European countries, including curve construction, volatility cube interpolation and sensitivity analysis. I also study calibrations of various models for different asset classes, especially stochastic-Alpha-Beta-Rho (SABR) model and its variants.
